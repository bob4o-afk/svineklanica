# Pitch & "Roast" Playbook — 10 min + 5 min Q&A

> The jury gives **20% to Presentation & "Roast"** *and* **30% to Radical Critical Thinking** — and the pitch is where the radical thinking gets *felt*. A working tool with a flat pitch loses to a working tool with a sharp one. This is how you make the 10 minutes land.
>
> Format (from the reglament): **up to 10 min pitch + 5 min Q&A.** Jury = tech + design + journalism + civil society. The rubric literally rewards *"how sharp, funny, and well-argued the problem is presented"* and *"speaking with facts while provoking."*

---

## The core principle: **roast the system, not the slides**

Punk pitching ≠ being loud. It's **cold, specific, fact-backed contempt for something broken**, delivered with timing. The most punk thing in a room full of "tech for good" pitches is to *not* show a mission statement and a cute logo. Open with the wound.

> *"The system relies on your silence. Be loud."* — your own reglament. Quote it back at them.

---

## The 10-minute structure (rehearse to 9:00, leave buffer)

| Time | Beat | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| **0:00–0:45** | **The Cold Open** | One brutal, true fact. No intro, no names, no "hi we're team X." Just the wound. |
| **0:45–2:00** | **The Real Problem** | Name the *systemic* failure (not the symptom). Why it's broken on purpose. |
| **2:00–2:45** | **The Stakes** | Who pays for this? Make it the citizen in the room. |
| **2:45–6:30** | **THE LIVE DEMO** | Your tool, on **real Bulgarian data**, doing the damning thing. The heart. |
| **6:30–7:30** | **The Punch** | The single most shocking output your tool produced. Sit in it. |
| **7:30–8:30** | **How it works + it's real** | 60 seconds of tech credibility: real data, open source, runs locally. |
| **8:30–9:00** | **The Call to Action** | What a citizen/journalist does with this Monday morning. |
| **9:00–10:00** | **Buffer / breathe** | Never run to the buzzer. Silence is power. |

---

## Beat-by-beat

### 1. The Cold Open (0:00–0:45) — *earn attention in one sentence*
Walk up, let one beat of silence land, then a single fact:
> *"The law gives a Bulgarian institution 14 days to answer a citizen's question. We asked 30 of them. [pause] 19 never replied. Here they are."*

Rules for the cold open:
- **A number, a comparison, or a contradiction.** ("They published this on a Friday at 5 p.m.")
- **No team intro, no agenda slide.** Identify yourselves at minute 8, not minute 0.
- **It must be true and sourced** — you'll be quizzed in Q&A.

### 2. The Real Problem (0:45–2:00) — *bite the cause, not the symptom*
The rubric's 30% "Radical Critical Thinking" asks: *did you bite the real problem or just a symptom?* Say the systemic thing out loud:
> *"This isn't incompetence. Un-queryable PDFs aren't an accident — they're a choice. You can't audit what you can't search."*

### 3. The Stakes (2:00–2:45) — *make it the room's problem*
> *"Every one of these contracts is your money. €X per citizen of Burgas. You just don't get to see where it went — until now."*

### 4. THE LIVE DEMO (2:45–6:30) — *the 30% Technical score lives here*
- **Demo live on real data.** The reglament says it three times: *real data, not samples.* A recorded/mocked demo reads as a lie.
- **One clean narrative path**, rehearsed to muscle memory. Don't tour features — tell one story: *input → the tool works → the damning output.*
- **Have a fallback:** screen-record a perfect run as backup if the venue Wi-Fi dies (local-first stack helps here — you chose it for exactly this).
- **Show the receipts on screen** — the actual data row, the actual source URL. The credibility is the punk.
- **Narrate the shock, don't just click:** *"Watch what happens when I sort by who wins the most… there it is."*

### 5. The Punch (6:30–7:30) — *one image they'll remember at scoring*
Pick the single most damning thing your tool surfaces and **dwell on it**. The network graph lighting up. The 19 red rows. The station that voted 100%. Don't rush past it — let the jury feel it.

### 6. It's real & it's yours (7:30–8:30) — *credibility in 60s*
Rapid-fire, no waffle:
- *"Real data: [source], scraped/queried live."*
- *"Built in 48 hours, 100% open source — MIT, so the next person can keep fighting."* (Open-source is mandatory; say it as a value, not a checkbox.)
- *"Runs on a laptop. No cloud, no gatekeeper."* (Local-first = a punk virtue: nobody can shut it down.)

### 7. Call to Action (8:30–9:00) — *the 20% UX axis: an ordinary citizen can act*
End on agency, not despair:
> *"A journalist opens this tomorrow and has 5 leads by lunch. A citizen files a complaint with one click. The data was always public. We just made it impossible to ignore."*

## The "Roast" toolkit (how to be funny + sharp without being a clown)

- **Deadpan > shouting.** State the absurd fact flatly and let the room laugh. *"This dataset was last updated in 2019. The website calls it 'live data.'"*
- **Juxtaposition.** Put the official claim next to the real number on one screen. The gap is the joke.
- **Name the pattern, not the person.** *"We won't name them. We don't have to — the public record does."* (This is both punk AND your defamation shield — see guardrails.)
- **Callback.** Reuse your cold-open line at the end. *"19 never replied. Now there's a scoreboard."*
- **Punk quotes as spine.** Open or close with the reglament's own lines (*"Punk is the refusal to accept that broken is normal."*). Quoting their manifesto back at them shows you got the assignment.

## 🎵 The Soundtrack — punk is music, so use it

Punk is *defined* by music as the tool of rebellion — leaning into it is on-thesis, not gimmicky. Two layers:

**Bulgarian first (this jury will feel it most):**
- **"Мутри вън!"** ("Mafia, out!") — the actual rallying cry of the **2020 anti-government protests** (from Radev's July 2020 speech). Use it as your **closing line or a section header**. A real Bulgarian civic-punk moment beats any foreign borrow.
- **Хиподил** and other BG punk/rock — pick references *you* know land locally.

**International anthems (for energy / a walk-up sting):**
- **Rage Against the Machine — "Killing in the Name"** (*"Fuck you, I won't do what you tell me"*) — the literal anti-authority anthem.
- **Megadeth — "Peace Sells… But Who's Buying?"** / **"Symphony of Destruction"** (manufactured leaders).
- **Sex Pistols — "Anarchy in the U.K."**, **The Clash — "Know Your Rights"**, **System of a Down — "B.Y.O.B."**

**How to deploy:**
- A lyric as a **black-slide title** to frame a section (*"Know Your Rights" → the ЗДОИ scoreboard*).
- A few seconds of a track as you **walk up** to the cold open.
- Name a feature/mode after a song for the roast.

> ⚠️ **Licensing:** lyrics-as-text and a few seconds live in a non-commercial pitch = practically fine. **Do NOT embed copyrighted audio in the open-source repo** — reference textually/aesthetically instead.

## Slides (if any) — punk visual rules
- **Black background, monospace, one idea per slide.** No corporate gradients, no stock photos of diverse people pointing at laptops.
- **Big numbers, small words.** A slide that's just `19 / 30` in huge type beats a paragraph.
- **The tool IS the deck.** Spend pixels on the live demo, not on bullet lists. Glitch/terminal aesthetic matches the LiberHack brand (their site is literally glitch-styled).
- **Show real screenshots/URLs**, never lorem-ipsum mockups (the reglament explicitly mocks "beautiful content-free slides").

## Surviving Q&A (5 min) — where weak projects die

The jury includes journalists and civil-society people who *will* probe. Prep answers cold:

| Likely question | Your prepared answer |
|---|---|
| *"Is this data real / where's it from?"* | Name the exact source + that it's public + open re-use. Have the URL ready. |
| *"Isn't this just an anomaly, not proof?"* | **Agree first**, then reframe: *"Correct — we flag, we don't convict. We hand journalists a sorted list of where to look."* (Never over-claim fraud.) |
| *"Aren't you defaming people?"* | *"Only public records, only public-office holders in official capacity, every claim links to source. We surface patterns; conclusions are the viewer's."* |
| *"What's novel vs existing tools (Bivol)?"* | Credit the precedent, then your delta: live/local/usable-by-anyone/open-source. |
| *"Could this be abused / weaponized for disinfo?"* | Show your guardrail: sourced, factual, no de-anonymizing redacted data. Punk is fact-based. |
| *"Does it actually run, or is the demo faked?"* | Offer to run it again live, on a value *they* pick. Confidence = points. |

**Q&A rules:** Answer the question asked (don't filibuster). If you don't know, say *"We didn't get to that in 48h — here's how we'd do it."* Honesty scores; bluffing gets caught.

## Division of labor (3 people, last 4 hours)
- **Driver** runs the live demo (knows every keystroke, has the fallback recording).
- **Voice** delivers the cold open, problem, and punch (the team's sharpest talker).
- **Defender** owns the data provenance + handles the hostile Q&A (knows every source URL).
- All three rehearse the **full run twice** before presenting. Time it. Cut anything that isn't the wound, the demo, or the punch.

- [ ] Cold-open line written, true, and sourced
- [ ] Demo runs on **real BG data**, live, in one clean path
- [ ] Fallback screen-recording saved locally
- [ ] The single "punch" output identified and rehearsed

- [ ] Repo is public + MIT license file committed
- [ ] Runs offline on the laptop (Wi-Fi-independent)
- [ ] Timed to ≤ 9:00, with silence built in
- [ ] One reglament punk-quote woven in
